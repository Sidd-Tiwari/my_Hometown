Famous Foods of Varanasi

Kachori Sabzi

A classic Varanasi breakfast dish, Kachori Sabzi is made with crispy puris stuffed with lentils or spices, served with a spicy, tangy potato curry. It’s a local favorite often enjoyed with jalebi.

Tamatar Chaat

A spicy and tangy delight unique to Varanasi, Tamatar Chaat is made from mashed tomatoes, boiled potatoes, desi ghee, and Indian spices. It’s served hot and topped with crunchy namkeen.

Baati Chokha

A traditional rustic dish, Baati Chokha consists of baked wheat dough balls (baati) served with spicy mashed vegetables (chokha). It’s healthy, filling, and very popular in UP and Bihar.

Chena Dahi Vada

Unlike the typical urad dal version, Varanasi’s dahi vadas are made of soft chena (paneer), soaked in creamy curd, topped with chutneys and spices. A cooling snack with a twist!

Malaiyyo

A winter delicacy of Varanasi, Malaiyyo is made from dew-whipped milk froth flavored with saffron and cardamom. It literally melts in your mouth and is served in tiny clay pots.

Banarasi Paan

No Varanasi food journey is complete without the famous Banarasi Paan. A mix of betel leaves, areca nut, sweeteners, and spices, it’s a cultural symbol of hospitality and celebration.
